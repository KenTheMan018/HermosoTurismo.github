Chocolate Hills

Bohol

Explore Chocolate Hills in Bohol

The Chocolate Hills are the name given to the 1,268 conical hills found in Bohol, Philippines. The term "chocolate hills" was coined because of the brown color of the grass coverings on the hills. The geological formation of the mountains are believed to be products of continuous erosion, a tectonic uplift that began about three million years ago.

 

The Chocolate Hills of Bohol is a beautiful sight to behold. Located atop the mountains in the towns of Carmen and Sagbayan, the tourist attraction is at the northern extremity of the province of Bohol. It covers ten square kilometers, with a total area of 726 hectares. It's one of the most visited attractions in the Philippines. It attracts more than one million tourists each year.

 

 

Is Chocolate Hills a wonder of the world?

Bohol Chocolate hills is a very important attraction of the Philippines because of their, scientific importance, beauty, and uniqueness. They were declared as the 3rd most important monument of the Philippines by the National Committee on Geological Sciences. People of the Philippines consider it a very important part of their country.

 

How tall are the Chocolate Hills?

There are more than 1200 individual chocolate hills. These all are of different sizes and shapes. Mostly are cone-shaped or dome shaped. Sizes are according to the shapes from 30 to 59 meters high. The largest hill among all of them is 120 meters or 390 ft. Chocolate hills is a very famous tourist spot in Bohol that expands on almost 50 square kilometers. Bohol chocolate hills are perfect to visit with friends and family.

 

When should I go to Chocolate Hills?

Most parts of the Philippines have a tropical climate. However, the peak tourism season is from December to May which is the dry season. April is the ideal month to visit the country. This is the time of less rainfall and great experiences of swimming, snorkeling, and diving.

 

You have to visit Bohol during the driest time of the year from January to May. They have their iconic chocolate appearance whereas they become lush green in the rainy season. Tourists especially visit the site during the dry months to capture the iconic color of the chocolate hills in their camera.

 

If you are in the area I suggest checking out Chocolate Hills but if you are looking for something amazing there are many more to uncover around Bohol.
